**Mechas** or **mechs** are typically large, humanoid, piloted machines. They are usually intended for purposes such as combat or heavy lifting, and are thus usually heavily armored and have integrated weaponry.

## In popular culture

### In real life

Some bespoke real-world mechas have been created. These range from basic exoskeletons to enhance the pilot's strength or speed, such as the [Guardian XO] by Sarcos Robotics, to those attempting to recreate fictional mechas, like [Archax] by Tsubame Industries.

[Guardian XO]: https://spectrum.ieee.org/sarcos-guardian-xo-powered-exoskeleton
[Archax]: https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2023/10/you-can-pilot-your-own-15-foot-tall-mecha-robot-for-2-7-million/

### In media

Mechas are a staple of Japanese anime, the 1979 "real robot" mecha series _Mobile Suit Gundam_ arguably being where the concept originates.

Mechas have since appeared in several anime series, including future spin-offs of _Gundam_, _The Super Dimension Fortress Macross_, _Neon Genesis Evangelion_

The concept was copied by western media through the _Voltron_ series (originally an adaptop of two other mecha anime: _Beast King GoLion_ and _Armored Fleet Dairugger XV_), the _Transformers_ franchise, and the live-action film series _Pacific Rim_.

Video games featuring mecha include the eponymous Titans in the _Titanfall_ series, _BattleTech_ and it's several spin-offs, and the _Armored Core_ series.
